Fermented Soybean Paste Stew

Fermented Soybean Paste, more traditionally known as Deonjang jjigae is the epitome of Korean comfort food. It is a classic Korean stew packed with vegetables that is incredibly comforting, satisfying, and easy to make. This recipe is customizable so you can toss in any vegetables or proteins laying around, but potatoes are an essential ingredient!

Ingredients

  • 1 medium potato (peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes)
  • 1 medium onion (cut into 1/2-inch pieces)
  • 1 small zucchini (cut into 1/2-inch pieces)
  • 1 green Korean chili pepper (stemmed and chopped)
  • 4 minced garlic cloves
  • 4 large shrimp (shelled, washed, coarsely chopped)
  • 7 dried anchovies (gut removed)
  • 2.5 cups water
  • 5 tablespoons fermented soybean paste
  • 6 ounces medium-firm tofu (cut into 1/2-inch cubes)
  • 2 green onions (chopped)

Directions

  1. Combine potato, onion, zucchini, chilli pepper, garlic, and shrimp in a heavy pot.
  2. Wrap dried anchovies in cheesecloth and put into pot. Add water and cover.
  3. Cook over med-high heat for 15 mins until boil.
  4. Stir in soybean paste, mixing well. Cover and cook for 20 mins longer over med heat.
  5. Add tofu and cook for another 3 mins. Remove anchovy pouch and discard.
  6. Sprinkle with green onions and serve hot with a bowl of rice.
