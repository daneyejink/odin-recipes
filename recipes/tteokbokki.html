Tteokbokki Recipe

Tteokbokki is a chewy rice cake dish cooked in a red, spicy broth that is a Korean classic. This recipe is by a Maangchi, in which she shares a traditional version based around an ancovy base. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of cylinder shaped rice cakes
  • 4 cups of water
  • 7 large dried anchovies with removed head and intestines
  • 6 x 8 inch dried kelp
  • 1/2 cup gochujang
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 3 green onions cut into 3 inch long pieces
  • 2 hard boiled eggs (optional)
  • 1/2 pound fish cakes (optional)

Steps

  1. Add water, dried anchoives, and dried kelp to a shallow pot, boiling for 15 minutes over med high heat without lid.
  2. Combine gochujang, gochugaru, and sugar in a small bowl.
  3. Remove anchoives and kelp from pot and add rice cakes, the mixture, green onion, fish cakes, and boiled eggs into the pot. The stock will be about 2.5 cups.
  4. Stir gently when it starts to boil. Then, let it simmer and keep stirring until the rice cakes soften and the sauce thickens and shines. This should take about 10 to 15 minutes. If the rice cakes are not soft enough, add more water and continue stirring.
  5. Remove from heat and serve hot.
