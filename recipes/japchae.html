Vegetable Japchae Recipe

Japchae is a sweet and savory stir-fried noodle dish consisting of an assortment of vegetables and meat (optional). It is served at special occasions and prepared in large quantities, as it is meant to be shared. This recipe takes no shortcuts, as each component is cooked and seasoned individually for the best and cleanest flavor. Enjoy!

Ingredients

Noodles, Vegetables, and Mushrooms

  • 8 ounces sweet potato starch noodles (soak in cold water for 40 mins)
  • 7 or 8 dried wood ear mushrooms (washed & soaked in cold water for 30 mins)
  • 8 ounces king oyster mushrooms (sliced into long strips)
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 1 large carrot (cut into 2-inch long and 1/4 inch wide matchsticks)
  • 8 ounces bunch spinach, washed, roots cut off, cut into 4-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil

Seasoning Sauce

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/2 to 1 teaspoon ground pepper
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced

Directions

  1. Drain wood ear muhsrooms. Cut off tough stems and cut caps into bite-size pieces. Add mushrooms to pot.
  2. Add king oyster mushrooms, carrot, and onion to pot.
  3. Add 1/4 cup vegetable oil and 1/4 cup water to pot. Mix everything well with both hands so everything is nicely coated with oil. (This prevents ingredients from browning from the sauce, but also burning when cooking)
  4. Spread the spniach over top in even layer.
  5. Drain noodles and cut them into 5- to 6- inch lengths with scissors. Place them on top of spinach in pot.
  6. Drizzle the seasoning sauce on top of the noodles.
  7. Cover and cook for 10 mins over med-high heat.
  8. Open lid, stir, and gently toss all ingredients with wooden sppons and tongs for 1-2 mins, until liquid has evaporated and noodles are cooked/shiny.
  9. Garnish with sesame seeds and serve. To reheat, stir-fry in a skillet with few tablespoons of water or oil. Enjoy!
